CNC 1.0 Build Notes

Step by step build notes. Click on the images to open up more detailed diagrams/models.

Front View

Ensure rail span is as per the image before cutting the gantry back.

Fitting the x rail to the base

Use the pre-drilled SBR20 holes as a guide.
Use some spare aluminium angle as a spacer.
Use a diamond coated holesaw to make the holes in the slate base. Slow and steady wins the day!

Drill holes in ballscrew mount (floating end)

Bearing block BF12/15 will be aligned to the corner of the angle aluminium.
The inner face of the aluminium angle will be aligned to the end of the table base.
The floating end bearing will be the anchor point to the base/x-rail. The fixed end and Nema motor will be a function of the ballscrew length.

Drill holes in ballscrew mount (fixed end)

1) Align aluminium angle face to Nema Motor mounting face
2) Affix ballscrew into fixed bearing BK15 and mate with Nema motor shaft using the appropriate coupler.
3) Mark and drill holes to fix the fixed bearing and the Nema motor to the aluminium angle
4) Ensure ball nut is on the ballscrew shaft, with ball nut mount attached. Affix ballscrew into floating bearing, and affix floating bearing onto the aluminium angle
5) Mark and drill holes to fix the aluminium angle mount onto the base, using the x rail screws
6) Attach entire x ballscrew assembly onto the underside of the base

Cut Gantry Sides

Machine the gantry sides out of stable 18mm playwood (or your preferred material) according to the specs in the image above.
Repeat for both sides.

Cut x Drive Arm

Machine the x drive arms out of stable 18mm playwood (or your preferred material) according to the specs in the image above.
Repeat for both sides.

Cut Gantry Back

Machine the gantry back out of stable 18mm playwood (or your preferred material) according to the specs in the image above.
Repeat for both sides.

Attaching the gantry back to the gantry sides

Align gantry back with one of the gantry sides such that the gantry back spans the full width.
Drill 6mm holes into the gantry back at frequencies specified in the diagram, offset from the edge by half the gantry side thickness.
Use a dowelling jig or similar to drill matching holes in the gantry side edges. Drill holes in the gantry side faces for the dowel buts to fit into.

Attaching gantry side angle

Mark and drill holes for the gantry side angle to act as a bracket between the gantry back and gantry sides.
Ensure adequate clearance from the various gantry side, y-rail, and y ball screw bearing holes.

Attach y rails and gantry back stiffener

1) Attach the top y rail flush with the top of the gantry back's inner face. Tighten screws firmly.
2) Align the bottom y rail with the lower insid edge of the gantry back's inner face. holes should be drilled1mm oversize into the gantry back, and at this stage screws should be finger tight.
3) Attach and tighten gantry back stiffening angle.

Drill holes for y Nema motor mount and fixed side ballscrew bearing

1) Align fixed side bearing (BK12) of the y ballscrew on the gantry back, flush with the inner gantry side face, and a spacer's width above the y rail.
2) Mark and drill 4 mounting holes into the gantry back, and temporarily hold the bearing block in position.
3) Align and mark holes in the y motor mounting bracket. To match the Nema motor mounting holes, and the BK12 mounting holes.

Drill holes for the floating side y ballscrew bearing

1) Assemble the BK12, the RM16 ballscrew, the ballnut and ballnut mount, and the BF12 floating side bearing.
2) Hold in place, referenced to the freshly drilled BK12 mounting holes, and mark and drill mounting holes for the BF12 floating bearing.
3) Mount the y ballscrew assembly and Nema motor/mount and tighten screws.

Electronics

Setup notes for electronics - notably the RPi/arduino interfaces, and the bcnc software setup.

GRBL steps/mm settings

RM1605 and RM2005 have a 5mm lead. This means they produce linear travel of 5mm per turn of the ball screw. The gCode settings which relate directly to this are $100 (x steps/mm), $101 (y, steps/mm), $102 (z, steps/mm). The final setting will also depend on how much microsetting there is.
For example, a 5mm lead on a ball screw means that one complete revolution of the ball screw moves the ballnut 5mm. If the stepper motor has 200 steps per revolution (1.8 degrees per step), then for a 5mm lead ball screw, that implies a gCode setting of 200/5 = 40 steps/mm. That implies a resolution of 1/40 = 0.025 mm.
For further details on how to set up the cnc shield for microstepping, see the Protoneer blog.
